Action System

This game is played turn-based, with turn order determined at the start of the battle with an Initiative roll. The actions you can take on your turn are as follows.

  • Standard Action: This action occupies most of your turn, and you can perform one per turn.

    • Example: The Attack Action is the most common, allowing a series of smaller attacks up to your Attack Speed, executed in any order. Refer to the "Attack" section for more details.

  • Swift Action: This action can be executed during any other action, with a limit of one per turn.

  • Free Action: These actions are effortless or part of other actions, allowing you to perform as many as you wish per turn - within reason.

  • Full-Round Action: This action requires the use of your standard action and all of your free Movement. You may still move if you have an ability that allows extra movement as a different action.

When it's not your turn, you can take the following actions:

  • Immediate Action: A Swift Action that can occur off-turn. Limited to one per round, shared with Swift Action â€” you cannot use both in the same round.

  • Instant Action: A Free Action that can occur off-turn. Referred to as "Instantly" in abilities.

    • Attack of Opportunity: A response triggered by certain actions of others. Limited to one per round. When a creature "Provokes," they are engaging in an action that allows others to AoO them.

  • Movement: You can move up to your Speed on your turn. Some abilities or effects may require spending a certain amount of your movement for the turn. You may have other forms of movement that manifest in terms of different Speeds - you may use multiple forms of movement on the same turn, but only up to the maximum amount for the given speed. Any slower speeds used overlap - That is, if you have a Fly 40 and a ground speed of 30, you may walk 30 feet and then fly another 10.
